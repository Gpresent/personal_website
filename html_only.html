About me

Hi, I'm Cole Broberg. I grew up in Dallas, TX, in a neighborhood called Lakewood (it is, indeed, by a lake). I took a liking to STEM very early on, getting involved with TMSCA Math Team in fourth grade and winning the school-wide math competition every year. In middle school, my experience with Math Team reached its height under a great coach and mentor of mine, Juan Fernandez.

By the time I entered high school, while I chose to enter the International Baccalaureate program, I knew that I wanted to work in the STEM world. My experience with coding was minimal until I hit high school, when (in 10th grade) I took AP Computer Science. The class opened my eyes to the possibilities offered by the field of computer science and the infinite creativity that coding can unleash. My school, Woodrow Wilson High School, didn't offer any prograssion beyond AP Comp Sci, but I was certain that I wanted to pursue it in university.

As a Engineering Honors student at Texas A&M, I've now taken Computer Science classes from the 100 to the 400 level, become a peer teacher for intro to C++, Discrete Mathematics, and Data Sctructures and Algorithms, begun research on graph algorithms with professor Tim Davis, and found many wonderful friends and comrades along the way.

I'm now a junior in Computer Science, the bug (pun intended) to get industry experience is pulling tighly at me, and I'm itching to see how the skills I've honed in the classroom and out of it will allow me to thrive in the workforce. I have three semesters remaining before graduation from A&M, and I'm planning on rounding out the next two years with an internship and co-op. My Computer Science journey is just beginning, and there's always more to learn. I'm looking forward to every moment of it.
